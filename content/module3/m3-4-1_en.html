Generic Valuation Tools

In developing your information classification systems, your toolbox should include generic valuation tools (GVTs). GVTs are documents that list recommended Information of Business Value for activities that are common across the Government of Canada; they also provide recommendations on the retention periods for these resources.

There are two kinds of GVTs: one for internal services and one for common operational activities

.  Activities that are unique to a department or not done by a sufficient number of departments are also candidates for GVTs. True or false?

  Correct! Activities that are unique to a department, not prescribed and predictable or done by a sufficient number of departments, are not candidates for GVTs.

  Incorrect. Activities that are unique to a department, not prescribed and predictable or done by a sufficient number of departments, are not candidates for GVTs.
