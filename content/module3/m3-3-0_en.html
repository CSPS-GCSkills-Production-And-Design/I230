Benefits of Function-Based Classification

Function-based classification is the approach that meets the needs of a modern age where information resources exist mainly in digital format. Using function-based classification allows departments to describe the content, context, and structure of their information holdings and supports four fundamental objectives.

Select each section below to learn more about the objectives.

  • 1. To establish context

    Establishing context ensures that information resources can be understood within their administrative and operational environments.

  • 2. To provide access and use

    Information is most valuable when it is easily accessible and available to support a variety of business needs.

  • 3. To facilitate retention and disposition

    Information resources organized by function and activities are maintained for the established period of time and can be disposed of according to the approved disposition schedule.

  • 4. To provide evidence

    Information resources are kept to document decisions and work activities to provide evidence that a department is meeting its mandate.

For a better understanding

For a better understanding

When information resources are described in sufficient detail, it becomes easier to understand their relationship to other related information resources and to related business processes and activities.

Using standards to describe information resources supports the capture and maintenance of information resources that are reliable and authentic. Having reliable and authentic information available to support work activities facilitates the achievement of goals and is the basis of good decision making.

A word from an expert

A word from an expert

Function-based classification is the approach recommended by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) as the standard for records management.
