Process and Steps

The process of creating your information classification systems involves seven steps. Select each step to learn more:

  • 1. Plan
    • Decide what type of file plan your department will need: for the function-based system, you will need to familiarize yourself with your department’s legislation and mandate.
    • Determine where to implement your file plan, for example, your designated corporate repository such as GCDOCS.
    • Identify key players and partners to ensure their engagement and buy-in.
  • 2. Research and analyze
    • Review your department’s mandate, mission and business activities: this will enable you to start brainstorming on the creation of the file plan.
    • Analyze how the information resources support or document the activities, and analyze other relationships and dependencies: it will help you pinpoint where the information should be saved.
    • Determine what tools you can use to help in the creation of the file plan.
  • 3. Consult
    • Consult with all business areas within your department: engage employees as much as you can; have them explain in their own words what it is that they do and then use their language to create the file plan.
  • 4. Establish file plan
    • Start at a high level and then go into more specific terms: from a generic term, such as legislation, go into a specific activity.
    • Take into consideration the specifications requested by the business units during consultations.
  • 5. Get consensus and sign off
    • Go back to the business units and get their consensus: you might need to make some modifications or explain why you could not accommodate all of their requests.
    • Get sign-off from the CIO.
  • 6. Implement your file plan
    • Work closely with the team that manages the application of electronic documents and records management systems such as GCDOCS.
    • Ensure that business units know exactly where to file their information: offer advice and guidance, and training or coaching sessions on where to file their IRBVs.
  • 7. Review and maintain
    • Review your file plan on a regular basis: changes to a department’s mission or mandate, business process and procedures, risk profile, or other factors can affect it.
    • Maintain your file plan on a regular basis: add retention and disposition schedules to IRBVs in order to dispose of them once their retention period expires.
