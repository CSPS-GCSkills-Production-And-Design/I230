Business Process Mapping

Another useful tool for developing your information classification system is business process mapping. This is the process of identifying your departmental IRBVs based on:

  • The business functions;
  • Who is responsible for IRBVs; and
  • What policy or legislation IRBVs refer to.

Business process mapping will assist your department to become more efficient in maintaining its IRBVs, keeping an open-by-default mind frame, and being as transparent as possible to Canadians.

When creating your IRBV lists, you can use any spreadsheet to capture the following information:

Program Link the IRBV to your department’s policy and legislations and provide the context in which IRBVs are created.
Sub-program If there is a need to include additional columns for sub-programs, link the IRBV to your department’s policy and legislations.
Business Unit Identify the business unit that performs the business process and creates the IRBV, as well as the business unit responsible for managing the IRBV.
Business Process Identify the actions being taken which will produce the IRBV. More columns can be added to reflect the complexity of the process if necessary.
IRBV Identify the information resource that is created by the business process, by the document name or document type.
Repository Indicate the location of the IRBV (name or number of the repository) and provide a link to an inventory of corporate repositories that describe the type of repository, its location, function, and nature (electronic, paper, etc.).
Classification Structure For the IRBV identified, provide the file name and/or number in the classification structure to allow the information resources to be easily located.
Security Indicate the security classification profile of the IRBV so that information managers know the security profile of the IRBVs and get assurance that they are appropriately stored.
ATIP Identify any exemptions or exclusions from the Access to Information Act and the Privacy Act that may apply to the IRBV.
Retention Period Indicate the duration in months or years that inactive IRBVs are retained by the department.
Trigger Indicate the trigger that is the event or moment that marks the beginning of the retention period.
