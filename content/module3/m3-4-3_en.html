Business Process Mapping

Another useful tool for developing your information classification system is business process mapping. This is the process of identifying your departmental IBV based on:

  • The business functions
  • Who is responsible for IBV
  • What policy or legislation IBV refer to.

Business process mapping will assist your department to become more efficient in maintaining its IBV, keeping an open-by-default mind frame, and being as transparent as possible to Canadians.

When creating your IBV lists, you can use any spreadsheet to capture the following information:

Program Link the IBV to your department’s policy and legislations and provide the context in which IBV are created.
Sub-program If there is a need to include additional columns for sub-programs, link the IBV to your department’s policy and legislations.
Business Unit Identify the business unit that performs the business process and creates the IBV, as well as the business unit responsible for managing the IBV.
Business Process Identify the actions being taken which will produce the IBV. More columns can be added to reflect the complexity of the process if necessary.
IBV Identify the information resource that is created by the business process, by the document name or document type.
Repository Indicate the location of the IBV (name or number of the repository) and provide a link to an inventory of corporate repositories that describe the type of repository, its location, function, and nature (electronic, paper, etc.).
Classification Structure For the IBV identified, provide the file name and/or number in the classification structure to allow the information resources to be easily located.
Security Indicate the security classification profile of the IBV so that information managers know the security profile of the IBV and get assurance that they are appropriately stored.
ATIP Identify any exemptions or exclusions from the Access to Information Act and the Privacy Act that may apply to the IBV.
Retention Period Indicate the duration in months or years that inactive IBV are retained by the department.
Trigger Indicate the trigger that is the event or moment that marks the beginning of the retention period.
