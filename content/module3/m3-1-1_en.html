Principles of Information Organization

Classification systems are designed to help organize and manage information resources of business value (IRBVs) to ensure their proper retention and disposition.

The four principles of an IM classification system are as follows:

  • Enable disposition
  • Be intuitive
  • Be cyclically updateable
  • Leverage existing tools

A word from an expert

Ongoing communication with users of the classification system is especially important at the planning, design, and implementation stages to encourage buy-in. Where user needs are not taken into consideration, the classification system could be disregarded. As a result, unauthorized parallel recordkeeping systems could be developed.
