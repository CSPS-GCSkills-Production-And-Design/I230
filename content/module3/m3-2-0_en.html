Principles of subject-based classification

The subject-based approach was the main classification system used when the majority of documents being filed were paper based.

Depending on the volume of information resources being handled, subject classification systems, like the alphabetical or numeric systems, may be used. Regardless of type, subject classification systems follow the same principles, as follows:

  • the subject of a record determines the primary number and subject (fundamental principle)
  • from the general to the specific
  • no speculative files
  • mnemonic method (use of significant numbers)
  • flexibility
