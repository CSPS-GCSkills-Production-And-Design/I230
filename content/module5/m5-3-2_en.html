Types of Disposition Authorizations

Disposition Authorizations (DAs) identify information of archival value and provide government departments with the authority to dispose of information resources that are no longer required for operational purposes.

There are two types of DAs:

  • Institution-Specific Disposition Authorities (ISDAs), issued to individual departments
  • Multi-Institutional Disposition Authorities (MIDAs), issued to groups of departments that carry out common activities

. Information resources determined not to have business or archival value, and required only for a limited time by a government department to ensure the completion of a routine action or the preparation of a subsequent information resource, are considered transitory information. True or false?

 Correct! Transitory information can be in published and unpublished form, regardless of communications source, information format, production mode or recording medium.

 Incorrect. The correct response is true. Transitory information can be in published and unpublished form, regardless of communications source, information format, production mode or recording medium.

RDACS includes descriptions of more than 2200 authorities and is available online.
