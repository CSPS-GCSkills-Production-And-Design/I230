Retention Specification Benefits

Retention specifications are established to facilitate the disposal of information resources once their business use has expired. The setting of retention specifications is an important and necessary step in managing IBV.

The key benefits of establishing retention specifications are:

  • Savings in operational costs
  • Savings in administrative costs
  • Risk mitigation

Can you name at least two other benefits of establishing retention specifications for your department? Provide your responses in the space below and then compare them with the suggested responses.

Suggested response: Other benefits include:

  • Cost effectiveness and business efficiency
  • Improved decision making
  • Ready access to the right records and evidence
  • Improved performance reporting
  • Increased alignment and support with transparency and accountability
  • Protection under audit, investigation, and litigation
  • Rational and defensible disposal of unnecessary information resources

For a better understanding

A smaller, more relevant collection of information resources results in less time and effort searching for information needed to support decision making, service delivery, and accountability. Information resources that are not needed on an immediate or regular basis (active versus inactive) can be moved from costly office space to more efficient off-site storage facilities or systems.
