What Is Disposition?

Disposition is the process that allows government departments to dispose of information resources that no longer have business value. LAC plays an important role in the disposition of government information and data because it provides departments with the authority to dispose of information resources.

. No information resource may be disposed of in the Government of Canada without the disposition authorities from LAC. True or false?

 Correct! The Library and Archives of Canada Act stipulates that “No government or ministerial record, whether or not it is surplus property of a government department, shall be disposed of, including by being destroyed, without the written consent of the Librarian and Archivist” [12(1)].

 Incorrect. The correct response is true. The Library and Archives of Canada Act stipulates that “No government or ministerial record, whether or not it is surplus property of a government department, shall be disposed of, including by being destroyed, without the written consent of the Librarian and Archivist” [12(1)].
