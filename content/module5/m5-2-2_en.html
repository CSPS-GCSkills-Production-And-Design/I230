Stakeholders

Once retention specifications have been developed, they must be formally approved by the chief information officer (CIO) designated to represent the deputy head to the Treasury Board of Canada Secretariat (TBS).

Select each section below to learn more about the key players in the process of developing retention specifications and the role they play.

  • Chief Information Officer
    • Ensures that the requirements and benefits of developing and implementing retention specifications are communicated to senior executives;
    • Ensures that all retention specifications are fully documented;
    • Approves plans for information resource retention specifications and disposition; and
    • Ensures the planning of a resourced approach to implementing the established retention specifications.
  • Managers
    • Lead or participate in developing retention specifications for information resources created or obtained for the business activities under their area of responsibility;
    • Provide the business content and context required for the research and analysis phase;
    • Participate in consultations with stakeholders to verify and obtain consensus on retention specifications for information resources in all repositories under their control; and
    • Recommend and obtain approval for retention specifications from the CIO.
  • Information Management Functional Specialists
    • Gain knowledge of valid disposition authorities and existing applicable retention specifications relevant to the information resource;
    • Collaborate with program managers during the process to gain knowledge of the legislative, policy, operational and audit impacts on retention specifications;
    • Document retention specifications and formulate the department’s information resource retention specifications and disposition plan;
    • Collaborate with IT functional specialists so that retention specifications for information resources in electronic repositories and applications are addressed effectively and efficiently;
    • Plan and coordinate the implementation of retention specifications; and
    • Review the retention specifications for possible amendments when legislation or policy is introduced or modified.

To assist Government of Canada departments in determining retention periods and developing retention schedules, TBS has prepared a document entitled "Setting Retention Specifications for Information Resources of Business Value”. This document, which is available on GCpedia, will assist you in determining retention periods for information resources of business value and also in developing retention schedules.
