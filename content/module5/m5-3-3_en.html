New Disposition Authorizations

LAC is currently transitioning to new disposition authorizations (DAs) to ensure that Government of Canada departments benefit from comprehensive disposition coverage under the Library and Archives of Canada Act.

Select each section below to learn more about LAC’s new approach to disposition.

  • New approach

    New DAs cover all activities carried out by a department, including internal administrative services such as human resource management and information management. Once a department receives a new DA, it will be able to apply a single authorization number to all of its records. New DAs will identify, at a high level, activities likely to generate records of archival value and require a validation exercise to determine the list of archival records. Records generated for all other activities may be disposed of immediately.

  • Two-step process

    Step 1: Government departments will provide an operational contact to facilitate access to departmental officials and records. This will allow LAC to develop a DA and an associated framework to identify what needs to be documented at the department’s strategic-function level.

    Step 2: Departments will submit the requested documentation to allow identification of the archival records from the DA. This is required only for those program areas where LAC believes records of historic and archival value are created.

  • New disposition package

    The new disposition package will have three key parts:

    1. A Disposition Authorization document, which is the legal authority to dispose of records (similar to recent disposition authorizations).
    2. An appendix “Disposition Framework,” which will outline the scope of disposition coverage, identify areas where archival records are created, and set out specific requirements for the disposition of records in those areas.
    3. Documentation from government departments that may be needed in relation to the requirements in the framework.

A word from an expert

A word from an expert

Until fully updated coverage is provided to a department, the current MIDAs and ISDAs applicable to that department will continue to be used. New MIDAs and corresponding frameworks will be developed and issued to groups of departments with similar mandates. For example, LAC has issued a MIDA for administrative tribunals.
