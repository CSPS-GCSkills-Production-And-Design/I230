Summary

In this module, you looked individually at all seven stages of the records and information life cycle.

Although activities and outcomes at each stage are unique, they all have something in common—they enable delivery of the programs and services of the Government of Canada.

Key points in Module 1

  • Remember to view information life-cycle management as one integrated process—as a cycle in which information lives.
  • This will allow you to identify the strengths and weaknesses of your departmental IM programs.
  • This will also allow you to define the risks and vulnerabilities that need to be documented to make the necessary improvements.
  • As IM professionals in your departments, you are expected to be proactively providing advice and assistance to program areas so as to facilitate the delivery of services to Canadians.

You may proceed to the next module, where you will learn about metadata and its role in information life-cycle management.
