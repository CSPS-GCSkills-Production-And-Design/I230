Products and Outcomes

Select each section below to learn more about the outcomes and products of stage 5 of the information life cycle.

  • Information Resources Risk Profile

    It provides a snapshot of the key risk areas for your department with regard to its information resources and offers mitigation and management strategies for those risks. Identifying and documenting risks to information resources is a key requirement of the Directive on Service and Digital.

  • Information and data are authoritative and trusted by all

    Stage 5 ensures the long-term availability of information and data and safeguards them from being maliciously altered or deleted before the end of their life cycle. This not only saves your department time and money, it also ensures decision makers have access to high-quality, authoritative information and data.

  • Preserving the documentary heritage of Canada for future generations

    The preservation component of stage 5 ensures your department is identifying information of archival value and taking the necessary steps to protect and transfer it to LAC in a timely manner.

  • Complete, accurate, and timely responses to Access to Information and Privacy (ATIP) requests

    Stage 5 ensures that appropriate procedures are in place to handle the security and preservation of information and data.
