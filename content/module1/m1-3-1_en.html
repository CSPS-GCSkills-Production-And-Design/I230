Principles of information organization Fundamentals of information organization
It is important that all information related to a given topic, subject, action or decision should be linked in some way so that it is easily accessible and reliable, no matter the format. The main principle of information organization means that all information on a particular subject will be easily accessible regardless of format. It is important that all information resources are identified properly (named and described), that they are well organized in a logical and systematic way, and that they are registered in an electronic inventory. Using metadata to describe and manage information resources, and to capture IBV in GCdocs, are examples of the fundamentals of information organization.
