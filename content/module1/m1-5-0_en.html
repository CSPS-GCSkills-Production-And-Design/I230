Stage 5: Maintenance, Protection, and Preservation

Good information management practices over time are critical to the maintenance, protection, and preservation of information resources. For example, having a designated corporate repository like GCdocs in place will give decision makers timely access to information, while at the same time protecting sensitive information from inadvertent disclosure, and information with archival or historical value from accidental loss or deletion.

A word from an expert

All Government of Canada departments are subject to strict legislation, policies, and procedures when it comes to the security and privacy of information. Therefore, as an IM professional, it is imperative that you take into consideration maintenance, protection, and preservation requirements when building and designing IM policies, procedures, systems, and tools.
