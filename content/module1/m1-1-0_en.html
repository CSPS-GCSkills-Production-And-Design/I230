Why Is This Stage Important?

Stage 1: Information Management Planning

Stage 1 is the stage where information management (IM) professionals and managers envision a desired future state for their IM program, translate this vision into broadly defined goals and objectives, and then outline a sequence of steps for their achievement. This stage ensures that IM requirements are addressed in departmental planning cycles and then integrated into the design of programs and systems.

Information is fundamental to the business of government. Having an IM plan will:

  • ensure that information resources are being properly managed at the outset of a project or initiative
  • help senior management determine where to spend money and resources to support your departmentâ€™s IM program

Good planning is key to the success of every initiative, large and small. A comprehensive IM plan can be associated with your entire department, a single program area, or simply a project or initiative.
