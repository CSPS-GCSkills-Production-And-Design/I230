Why is This Stage Important?

Stage 3: Organization

Organization is an important stage in information life-cycle management as it ensures information resources are structured according to set rules and requirements, in order to be retrieved and managed. These rules and requirements support the government’s business while taking its policies, procedures, mandates, and legislation into consideration.

.  Organizational rules and requirements prescribe the ways that information resources should be created, retrieved, and disposed of over time. True or false?

  Correct! Organizational rules and requirements prescribe the ways that information resources should be stored and handled over time.

  Incorrect. The correct response is false. Organizational rules and requirements prescribe the ways that information resources should be stored and handled over time. Creation, retrieval and disposition happen at other stages in information life-cycle management.
