Products and Outcomes

When preparing to dispose of records, consider input from the following:

  • Departmental IM strategy and plan, as well as other disposition-related policies, procedures, and plans;
  • Program areas or the office of primary interest (OPI) that created the information; and
  • Library and Archives Canada (LAC), which issues the legal authority to dispose of information.

The main outcome at this stage is the timely disposition of government information resources that no longer have business value. Typical products and outcomes include:

  • The transfer of archival records to LAC;
  • The timely disposition of information resources;
  • The transfer of control from the Government of Canada (alienation);
  • The transfer of surplus publications to LAC.
