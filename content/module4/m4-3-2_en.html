Search Steps

There are ten steps you can follow when conducting a search of a repository, database or search engine. Although they may not be applicable to every case, the steps can guide you in your search strategy. Select each step to learn more about it.

  • 1. Identify concept(s)

    Identify the important concepts of your search.

  • 2. Choose keywords

    Choose the keywords that describe these concepts (or use the words provided by the legal team, for litigation purposes).

  • 3. Choose synonyms

    Determine if there are synonyms, related terms or other variations on the keywords that should be included.

  • 4. Determine additional search features

    Determine which search features apply, including truncation, proximity operators, Boolean operators, etc.

  • 5. Choose search engine

    Choose a search engine.

  • 6. Read search instructions

    Read the search instructions on the search engine’s home page. Look for sections entitled “Help,” “Advanced Search” or “Frequently Asked Questions.”

  • 7. Create search expression(s)

    Create a search expression using syntax appropriate for the search engine.

  • 8. Evaluate results

    Evaluate the results. How many hits were returned? Were the results relevant to your query?

  • 9. Modify search

    Modify your search if needed. Go back to steps 2 and 4 and revise your query accordingly.

  • 10. Repeat with different search engine

    Try the same search in a different search engine, following steps 5 through 9.
