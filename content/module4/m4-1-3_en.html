Electronic Discovery

In case of special investigations or litigation, you should be aware of electronic discovery, or e-discovery. It deals with the exchange of all relevant information in electronic format, including, but not limited to, emails, documents, presentations, databases, voicemail, audio and video files, social media, web sites, as well as metadata.

The e-discovery process also includes the gathering, processing, analysis, and eventual production of material. There are various text analytics or text mining tools that can help with the processing and analysis of digital content. For a better understanding

The Sedona Conference Working Group on E-Discovery Issues in Canada was formed in 2006. The group developed ten principles to facilitate electronic discovery in Canada and to provide guidance in the resolution of e-discovery disputes. These are known as the Sedona Canada Principles. The Sedona Canada Principles Addressing Electronic Discovery are available online.

A word from an expert

A word from an expert

E-discovery is a legal requirement that affects information management, as electronic information increasingly contains critical material that may be related to legal actions, audits, or government investigations. Remember that e-discovery is a more complex process than looking for a file or documents for a client or an ATIP request because of the volume of electronic data produced and stored and the number of stakeholders involved.
