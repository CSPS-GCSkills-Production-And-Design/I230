Advanced Search

When conducting an advanced search, you can use different operators or wildcards to either narrow or broaden your search results. The operators AND, OR and NOT are used most often.

.  Which of the following search strategies can narrow or focus your search?

  Correct! The operator AND will narrow your search as your search results will include documents that contain both terms.

AND, OR and NOT are the three Boolean operators. The term Boolean is named for the man who invented this system, George Boole.

Truncation is the search technique used to find just a portion of or variations on a word. Wildcard searching allows the substitution of one or more characters within a word to expand a search. The number of characters and the symbols used will vary depending on the search engine and the database.
