Print Table

And

For example: copyright AND moral rights. Document must have both words (or both phrases).

If you are retrieving too many records on your search topic, try adding another search term with the connector AND. You will narrow your search because search results will include all documents that contain both the first term and the second term.

Or

For example: tobacco OR cigarettes. Document can have either word (or either phrase).

If you are retrieving too few records on your search topic, try adding another search term with the connector OR. You will expand your search because search results will include all documents that contain either the first term or the second term or both.

Not

For example: search NOT find. Document must have first term; must not have second.

If you are retrieving too many records on an unrelated search topic, try eliminating a word with the connector NOT. You will exclude an idea or concept from your search because it will find documents that contain the first word, but do not contain the second.

Adj

For example: obsessive adj compulsive. This will force the computer to search for words in a specified order.

You will find results where the first term “obsessive” immediately precedes the second term “compulsive.”

Near

For example: alcohol near abuse / alcohol near3 abuse. This will retrieve items that have both terms in the same sentence. A number added to “near” will instruct the computer to find results within that number of words in any order.

This will retrieve results where the first term “alcohol” is within 3 words of the second term “abuse” (”Men who abuse their wives after alcohol consumption” or “Alcohol consumption leads to abuse”).

FBY

For example: search fby retrieve / search fby5 retrieve. Followed by.

This will retrieve results where the first term “search” is followed by the second term “retrieve” in 5 words.

* (asterisk)

For example: critic* .This truncation will pick up variations of a word stem.

Asterisks can represent zero or more terminal characters in a search term (critic* will find critic, critics, criticism, critical, etc.).

? (question mark)

For example: wom?n This will instruct the computer to search for and match all letters in a word and use any letter to replace the wildcard symbol.

Question marks replace any character in a given search term, regardless of position (wom?n will retrieve records with woman and women).
