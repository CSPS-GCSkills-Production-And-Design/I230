What Is Metadata?

Metadata is structured information that describes, explains, locates or facilitates the retrieval, use or management of an information resource. In other words, metadata is information about information.

Metadata is everywhere! It’s in your department’s HR and financial systems, a library catalogue, your local grocery store, the corner bookstore, and on the Internet.

For a better understanding

Here are some examples of commonly recorded metadata:

  • Person: author name, project participants, user name, sender, contact person, etc.
  • Resource description: title (of a resource, a file, a folder, etc.); resource type (briefing note, procedure, agenda, etc.); subject/keywords (words that describe content); unique identifier (project or contract number, employee number, etc.); format (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, AutoCAD, etc.)
  • Place: location where information resource was published, where research was done, where head office is located, Geographic Information System (GIS) coordinates, etc.
  • Event: date information resource was created, received or modified; period of time covered (fiscal year, calendar year, 1990s, etc.)
