Summary

In this module, you learned what metadata is and how it is used in managing information in the Government of Canada.

Key points in Module 2

  • Metadata uses specific information to describe, identify, and enable the access and sharing of information resources.
  • Over time, metadata helps preserve the authenticity, reliability, usability, and integrity of information resources.
  • Standardization facilitates the consistent use of metadata so as to minimize the risk of loss and duplication of IRBVs.
  • As an IM specialist, you support end users by ensuring that the metadata needed to meet the business and functional requirements of your department is gathered.

You may proceed to the next module to learn about the third step in the information life cycle management and classification and organization systems.
