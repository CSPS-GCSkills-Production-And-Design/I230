A word from an expert

A word from an expert

Metadata serves the same function as a label on a tin can. A sealed and unlabelled tin could contain anything: motor oil, baked beans, or cat food. The only way to know what is inside is to open the tin. But a label on the tin tells you what is inside the can and lets you decide whether or not to buy it.

The TBS metadata standards are similar to Canadian product labelling regulations: they require that federal government departments record certain mandatory information (the metadata), expressed in a standardized way, to identify and access its information resources.
